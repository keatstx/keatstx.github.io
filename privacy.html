Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy explains how Glowline Creative LLC (“Glowline,” “we,” “our,” or “us”) collects, uses, and shares information in connection with our website and Services.

2. Sources

We collect information directly from you, automatically through our site and tools, and from third‑party platforms you connect or grant access to, including ad platforms, analytics, and CRMs.

3. How We Use Information

  • Provide and improve the Services, including creative development, campaign management, and reporting.

We and our partners use cookies, pixels, and analytics tools to operate the site, remember preferences, measure performance, and deliver or retarget ads. You can control cookies through browser settings. Blocking cookies may impact site features.

5. Sharing of Information

  • Professional advisors such as lawyers and accountants.
  • Legal and safety when required by law or to protect rights, safety, or security.

6. PHI and HIPAA

We do not require protected health information (“PHI”) to provide the Services. If PHI processing is necessary, we will execute a Business Associate Agreement (BAA) and restrict use to the minimum necessary. Do not send PHI through website forms or unsecured channels.

7. Your Choices

8. Data Security and Retention

We use reasonable administrative, technical, and physical safeguards to protect information. No method is 100 percent secure. We retain information for as long as needed for the purposes described or as required by law.

9. International Data Transfers

If you access the site from outside the United States, your information may be transferred to and processed in the U.S. where laws may differ from those in your jurisdiction.

10. Children’s Privacy

Our site and Services are intended for business users. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13.

11. State and Regional Disclosures

If you reside in a region with privacy rights such as the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA/CPRA) or the EU/UK GDPR, you may have rights to access, correct, delete, or restrict processing of personal information, and to opt out of certain sharing or targeted advertising.
